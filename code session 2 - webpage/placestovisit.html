Places To Visit

Durham Castle

Location: Durham University, Palace Green, Durham City, County Durham, DH1 3RW

Durham castle (and Durham Cathedral) is a World Heritage Site; awarded in 1986 for its unique, historic and scenic area. It was built during the Norman period as a fortress, which turned into a palace during the Norman Conquest and the English Civil War, and then changed again into an institution during the Victorian period.

The Auckland Project

Location: Market Place, Bishop Auckland, County Durham, DL14 7NR

The Auckland Project consists of a historical town market with heritage attractions, galleries, gardens and parkland located in Bishop Auckland, in the centre of County Durham. Bishop Auckland was a home for Prince Bishops of Durham, with a history that spans over 2,000 years. It has attractions including Auckland Tower, Auckland Palace, the Mining Art Gallery, the Spanish Gallery, the Walled Garden and the Deer Park. The Auckland Palace is built on 150 acres of parkland, built by England's only Prince Bishops as their own private hunting ground over 800 years ago.

Egglestone Abbey

Location: Abbey Lane, Barnard Castle, County Durham, DL12 9TN

Located above the River Tees, about a mile southeast of Barnard Castle. A small monastery of Premonstratensian 'white canons', it includes a church on the south side of the cloister dating back to the 13th century and living quarters, as well as some toilet drainage system.

Bowes Castle

Location: Bowes, Barnard Castle, County Durham, DL12 9HP

The castle was built between 1171 and 1187 on the site of the Roman fort of Lavatris, sitting in a field surrounded by a moat on two sides. It was built to guard the route known as the Stainmore Pass and was occupied from the last 1st century to the late 4th century. The keep is the only part of the castle that remained, which was built by Henry II, who recognised the route's importance and its crucial defensive position.

Ushaw Historic House, Chapels, Gardens

Location: Woodland Road, Durham, County Durham, DH7 7DW

When England became a Protestant country during the reign of Henry VIII and Elizabeth I, Catholics were exiled to Northern France and lived in Douai for 300 years. And during the French Revolution, they were forced out of Douai and returned to England in the 19th century.

Raby Castle, Park and Gardens

Location: Raby Castle, Staindrop, Darlington, County Durham, DL3 3AH

Built in the 14th century by the Neville family, home to Cecily Neville, mother of two kings of England. It was also the place of the plotting of the Rising of the North and a Parliamentary stronghold during the Civil war. Today, visitors get to witness this medieval fortress surrounded by parklands roamed by deer, and it had gone through restorations to be remodelled.

Barnard Castle

Location: Barnard Castle, County Durham, DL12 8PR

A fortress sitting above the Auckland Project market town, overlooking the River Tees, dating back to the 12th century, a home to the Balliol family whom the Castle was named after (Barnard de Balliol. The remains include a 14th century great hall, a 3-storey keep and a round tower. It was confiscated when John de Balliol was overthrown by Edward I in 1216. And in 1630, the fortress was taken apart to relocate the belongings and furniture to Sir Henry Vane's new Raby Castle.
